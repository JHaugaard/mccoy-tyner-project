# McCoy Tyner Project — Initial Questions

Project directory: `~/dev/active/mccoy-tyner/`

## Overview
Compile canonical jazz albums across three phases (Cool Jazz, Hard Bop, Modal Jazz), extract personnel, and build an interactive exploration layer.

## Questions to Settle

### 1. Data Source Philosophy
- Curated expert lists as starting point (e.g., Penguin Guide, DownBeat, jazz historians)?
- Or research and compile from multiple sources?
- Other approach?

**Answer:** We have the time and resources. Let’s be as comprehensive are reasonably possible. The published works your mention will certainly be a start; there are probably hundred of “Best of ” lists out there. I expect that this collection, sorting, and evaluation phase will take the longest. It will give me a chance to read, dig, and evaluate. As well as listen. (Thank goodness for Apple Music.)
It may be that as we dig in, the task of assembling turns out to be easier then we think. Then the fun will be to sort at the margins, to my tastes. 
So we’ll turn to the heavy-hitters of jazz criticism, as well as look in the nooks and crannies. Are you in?

---

### 2. Scope Boundary
- Are Cool Jazz / Hard Bop / Modal Jazz *exclusive* categories (albums must fit one)?
- Or can an album bridge phases (e.g., Miles Davis spans all three)?
- How do we handle transition albums?

**Answer:** I don’t want to categorize the albums with in the 100. I’m using the 3 style labels as a proxy for a time range and a broad style range. I could have said 1940 - 1969. But that would not get at the heart of the list I want. So if an album is too Bee-Bop, then not in. If the album is on the shoulder with elements of both - then a candidate. But I want the jazz that came after be-bop and was recorded generally up to the Fusion period (think Bitches Brew). And, I’m not interested, as least for now in Free Jazz.  So that’s why I used the characterization. No hard criteria on numbers or personnel. I would expect Miles Davis to show up across the board. McCoy Tyner, too. If there are 60 Modal Jazz albums, great. No quotas or number balance required. And since we aren’t categorizing by style the question of transition albums goes away. They are what they are.

## Notes
Why mccoy-tyner. I listen to a lot of jazz, but don’t always know the details of what is playing. So I often make a stab at who is playing that solo, etc. It seems that on a majority of cases I’ll say to my wife, “I think that’s McCoy Tyner!” And I’m always pleased when I turn out to be correct.
